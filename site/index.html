A daily six-word story game

Six words. One prompt. The whole day's wall.

Sestet is for people who would rather make something small than solve a puzzle. Everyone who shows up today gets the same prompt. You write exactly six words, hit submit, and land in a live wall of everyone else's take on the same six words. Read them, upvote the ones that land, done before midnight.

Why it's worth a minute a day

One prompt for everyone

Every visitor on the same day sees byte-identical prompt text. You are writing against the same six words as everyone else, right now, not into an empty box.

A wall that feels live

Submit and you are in it instantly. Lines from other people slide in on their own with a soft glow, no refresh, while you sit and read.

Votes that resist gaming

One entry and one vote per browser, enforced by database constraints rather than a check in the UI. A double-click or a retried request cannot pad the count.

A midnight close and archive

At the UTC day's end the wall freezes and a new prompt begins. Past days live on in a read-only archive, each sorted by the votes its lines earned.

Questions

What is a six-word story?

A complete story told in exactly six words. The form is often traced to Hemingway's "For sale: baby shoes, never worn." The whole craft is fitting a beginning, middle, and turn into six words, which is why it is approachable: having an idea is the entire task.

Do I need an account?

No. There is no sign-up and no login. Sestet keeps a single random token in your browser so it can enforce one entry and one vote per person. Clear your cookies and you are a fresh anonymous visitor.

How often does the prompt change?

Once per day, at midnight UTC. Everyone in the world gets the same prompt for that day. When the day rolls over, the wall freezes into the archive and a new prompt opens for the next round.

Can I submit more than one story a day?

One per day, on purpose. The single-entry limit keeps the wall honest and makes your six words count. You can upvote as many other entries as you like.

Is it open source?

Yes, MIT licensed. It is a Next.js app running on Cloudflare's edge with D1 and KV. The code, the design notes, and the architecture writeup are all on GitHub.
